Barber Boss Exeter Privacy Policy – Your Data, Clearly Protected

Last updated: 15 February 2026

At Barber Boss Exeter, the protection of your personal data is our highest priority. This privacy policy aims to clearly explain how we collect, use, share and protect your information when you use our website, with particular attention to users in the United Kingdom. We are committed to complying with the UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR) and applicable British data protection laws.

What data we collect and how it fuels your experience

To provide you with the best possible services, we may collect the following types of data:

  • Personal information provided voluntarily: When you create an account, we collect your name, email address, phone number and other information necessary for providing our services.
  • Browsing data: We collect information about your interaction with our site, including IP address, browser type, pages visited and duration of visits.
  • Location data: With your consent, we may collect information about your location to personalise the services offered.
  • Payment information: Financial transactions are handled by secure payment providers and we do not store your card details.

This data enables us to:

  1. Personalise your user experience according to your preferences

  2. Continuously improve our services and features

  3. Provide you with effective and personalised customer support

  4. Ensure the security of your account and prevent fraud

  5. Inform you about relevant news and offers (with your consent)

The essential role of cookies in personalising Barber Boss Exeter

Cookies are small text files stored on your device that enhance your experience on our site. We use different types of cookies:

Cookie Type Purpose Retention Period
Essential cookies Basic site functionality (login, basket) Session or 12 months
Performance cookies Analysis of site usage and improvement 24 months
Functional cookies Remembering your preferences 12 months
Targeting cookies Personalised advertising (with consent) 12 months

You can control cookies through your browser settings. However, disabling certain cookies may limit some functionalities of our site.

How we responsibly use your data to improve our services

We use your data in strict accordance with the purposes for which it was collected:

  • Contract performance: To provide the services you requested and manage your account.
  • Legitimate interest: To improve our services, prevent fraud and ensure platform security.
  • Consent: To send you marketing communications when you have explicitly agreed to receive them.
  • Legal obligation: To comply with applicable UK and European laws and regulations.

We process your data only for as long as necessary to fulfil the purposes for which it was collected, in accordance with UK legal requirements.

Strict protocols for sharing data with trusted partners

We do not sell or rent your personal data to third parties. We may share your information with:

  1. Service providers: Partners who help us operate our site (hosting, analytics, payment) under strict confidentiality.

  2. Legal authorities: When required by UK law or in the context of legal proceedings.

  3. Business partners: Only with your explicit prior consent.

All our partners are carefully selected and contractually obliged to protect your data in accordance with UK GDPR. In case of transfer outside the UK, we ensure appropriate safeguards are in place.

Your rights to access, modify or delete your data at any time

In accordance with UK GDPR, you have the following rights regarding your personal data:

  • Right of access: Obtain confirmation that we are processing your data and access it.
  • Right to rectification: Request correction of inaccurate or incomplete data.
  • Right to erasure: Request deletion of your data in certain cases.
  • Right to restriction: Request restriction of processing in certain situations.
  • Right to object: Object to processing for legitimate reasons.
  • Right to data portability: Receive your data in a structured format.

To exercise these rights, contact our Data Protection Officer (DPO) at dpo@barberbossexeter.co.uk. We will respond within one month, in accordance with UK regulations.

Keeping you informed – timely updates to privacy policies

We are committed to informing you of any material changes to this privacy policy. Updates will be published on this page with an indication of the last revision date. For significant changes, we will notify you by email or via a visible notice on our site.

We encourage you to review this policy regularly to stay informed about how we protect your information. Your continued use of our services after any changes constitutes acceptance of the updated terms.
